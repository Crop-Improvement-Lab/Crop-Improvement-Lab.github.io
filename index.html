About Our Lab

Our laboratory is interested in studying the mechanism of bacterial virulence and plant defense using Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae (Xoo) and rice as a model system. In the area of rice functional genomics, our group is working towards identification and characterization of genes involved in important agronomic traits in rice such as disease (bacterial blight, sheath blight) and pest (yellow stem borer) resistance, abiotic stress resistance, higher yield, early maturation etc. We are attempting to develop novel markers for some of these important agronomic traits using genetic methodologies, NGS and bioinformatic analysis. The novel markers will be useful for the development of newer rice varieties.

Our Achievements

Breakthrough in Disease Resistance

Successfully identified novel genetic markers for bacterial blight resistance in rice, accelerating disease-resistant crop development.

High-Yield Varieties

Developed rice strains with higher yield, helping farmers maximize productivity under abiotic stress conditions.

Innovative Gene Editing

Pioneered the application of CRISPR/Cas9 for targeted improvement of rice traits such as pest resistance and early maturation.
